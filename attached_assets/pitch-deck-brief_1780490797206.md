## 0. How to use this brief

- Every slide below is specified as: **Headline → Subhead → Body copy → Speaker notes → Visual / art direction → AI image prompt → Source of truth.** Lift the copy directly; adapt prose to the deck's final design system.
- **Brand voice:** confident, technical, grown-up. Say what the product _does_, then what it _delivers_. No buzzword soup, no emojis. Full voice rules and the vocabulary cheat-sheet live in `marketing-content-pack.md` § Appendix.
- **Positioning chosen for this deck:** a **horizontal platform told through a legal-firm lead example.** The platform story is universal; the legal firm is the recurring, concrete illustration so the deck stays reusable across other prospects. Where a slide uses the legal example, it is labelled _[legal example]_ and can be swapped per vertical.
- **The spine of the whole deck** is three verbs — **Connect → Understand → Act** — all wrapped in **Trust**. Every section ladders back to this. The live app walkthrough follows the same spine.
- **Length target:** 15–18 core slides + 2–4 optional appendix slides pulled up only on request. Keep the main flow tight; depth is on tap, not on display.

### Reusable AI prompt block (paste into your AI design/copy/image tools)

> You are producing slides for **Booga Agents**, an enterprise AI automation & analytics platform. Audience: senior **business** decision-makers (not engineers). Voice: confident, technical, grown-up; concrete over abstract; outcomes in headlines, features in bullets; no buzzword soup ("synergize", "leverage", "AI-powered solutions"); **no emojis**. Use the contrast pattern "Most software does X. Booga does Y." and the three-noun pattern ("Autonomous workflows. Enterprise control. Built for scale."). Vocabulary: say **agent** (not bot), **workflow** (not flow), **plugin** (not module), **tenant** (not customer in technical copy), **stack** (not environment). Citable facts: 600+ documented REST endpoints; compliance reporting for SOX, GDPR, HIPAA, ISO 27001, PCI DSS; 7-year default audit retention; tenant-isolated infrastructure (own database, own vector index, own region); multilingual document extraction. Keep claims grounded — do not invent numbers, customers, or capabilities beyond this brief.

### Art-direction north star

- **Look:** enterprise-grade, calm, high-contrast, lots of whitespace. Think "the platform your security team approves of," not "consumer AI startup."
- **Recurring motif:** the **Connect → Understand → Act** triad as a simple left-to-right flow, reused as a progress indicator across section dividers.
- **Colour:** Booga brand palette (agency to confirm from brand kit). Keep accent colour for the one number/word that matters per slide.
- **No stock-photo clichés** (robots, glowing brains, handshakes). Prefer clean product-UI shots, simple diagrams, and typographic slides.

### Slide 2 — Why now

- **Headline:** _AI is finally good enough to do the work. Most enterprises still can't use it._
- **Subhead:** The tools are powerful. The barrier is trust, control, and your data being everywhere but usable.
- **Body copy (3 short stats/points):**
  - Generic AI assistants don't know your business — and you can't safely show them.
  - Your most valuable knowledge is scattered across thousands of folders nobody can search.
  - "Build it ourselves" is a multi-year project most teams can't staff.
- **Speaker notes:** Frame the moment, not the technology. The prospect already believes AI matters; your job is to name _why they haven't acted_. This sets up the problem slide. Keep it to 30 seconds.
- **Visual / art direction:** Three-up icon row or a simple "gap" diagram: "AI capability" line rising, "enterprise adoption" line flat, the gap shaded.
- **AI image prompt:** _"Simple business line chart, two lines: one labelled 'AI capability' rising steeply, one labelled 'safe enterprise adoption' nearly flat, the gap between them shaded as a problem zone, clean minimal infographic style, no photos."_

### Slide 3 — The problem, in their language
