## 0. What this deck is — and isn't

- **Job to be done:** earn the meeting. This deck does **not** close, does **not** price, and does **not** go deep. It creates enough pull that the prospect replies "show me."
- **Read time:** under 60 seconds. **Length:** 5–6 slides, hard cap at 7.
- **Must stand alone:** there is no presenter. Every slide must make sense in silence. Headlines carry the message; body copy is minimal.
- **Same spine as the main deck:** Connect → Understand → Act, wrapped in Trust — but compressed to a single slide.
- **Format:** designed as a PDF that looks good both full-screen and as inline email images. Consider a matching 1-image "preview" for the email body that links to the full PDF.
- **No pricing. No emojis. No buzzword soup.** (Full voice rules: `marketing-content-pack.md` § Appendix.)

## 1. Slide-by-slide brief

### Slide 1 — Cover + value prop

- **Headline:** _Put your firm's knowledge to work — without it ever leaving your control._
- **Subhead:** Booga Agents — Enterprise AI Automation & Analytics Platform.
- **Micro-copy (one line):** Connect your documents. Booga organizes them. Agents do the work.
- **Visual / art direction:** Mirror the main deck cover for continuity. Bold typographic title, Booga logo, faint document-grid-to-nodes motif.
- **AI image prompt:** _"Minimal enterprise teaser cover: faint document/file grid resolving into clean connected nodes, dark calm palette, generous negative space, no text, no robots, premium B2B aesthetic."_
- **Source of truth:** `pitch-deck-brief.md` Slide 1.

### Slide 2 — The problem (sharp, one breath)

- **Headline:** _Your knowledge is your edge. Today it's trapped._
- **Body copy (three words + line each):**
  - **Trapped** — answers buried across thousands of folders.
  - **Expensive** — your best people drafting and searching by hand.
  - **Risky** — every workaround is an exposure.
- **Visual / art direction:** Three minimal cards (Trapped / Expensive / Risky), each one line icon. Identical to main deck Slide 3 for consistency.
- **AI image prompt:** _"Three minimal problem cards in a row with simple line icons: a tangled folder tree, an hourglass over a document, a document crossing a secure boundary, labelled Trapped / Expensive / Risky, muted enterprise infographic, no photos."_
- **Source of truth:** `pitch-deck-brief.md` Slide 3.

### Slide 3 — The whole platform on one slide

- **Headline:** _Connect. Understand. Act. All inside your own walls._
- **Body copy (one line each):**
  - **Connect** — plug into SharePoint and Microsoft 365. One-way. Read-only. Your source of truth never changes.
  - **Understand** — Booga reads and organizes every document automatically — even scanned, legacy, and multi-language files.
  - **Act** — agents draft replies, draft documents from your precedents, and pull in outside data — described in plain English.
- **Visual / art direction:** The hero Connect → Understand → Act diagram, enclosed in a "Trust" border. Reuse the main deck's Slide 4 visual exactly.
- **AI image prompt:** _"Horizontal three-step diagram labelled Connect, Understand, Act with simple line icons (connector, documents becoming an organized grid, an agent doing a task), enclosed in a rounded 'Trust' border with a subtle shield/lock motif, premium enterprise infographic, brand accent on one element, no photos."_
- **Source of truth:** `pitch-deck-brief.md` Slides 4–7.

### Slide 4 — One hero outcome _[legal example]_

- **Headline:** _A client email at 9:02. A drafted, grounded reply by 9:15._
- **Body copy:** An agent reads the email, finds the relevant matter history and precedents in your own archive, and drafts a reply for a lawyer to review and send. The expert decides; the agent does the legwork.
- **Visual / art direction:** A tight before→after, or a mini timeline. Show "drafted from your own documents, with citations." One screenshot-style mockup works well here.
- **AI image prompt:** _"A clean before-and-after panel: left an empty email reply, right a drafted reply with small citation chips referencing internal documents, a '9:02 to 9:15' time tag, premium enterprise UI mockup style, no real text needed, brand accent."_
- **Source of truth:** `pitch-deck-brief.md` Slides 7–8; user legal use cases.

### Slide 5 — Trust strip

- **Headline:** _Your data. Your stack. Your rules._
- **Body copy (one line):** Isolated by architecture, one-way and read-only at the source, audited end to end, governed access — the foundation, not an add-on.
- **Visual / art direction:** A quiet horizontal strip of four icons (isolation / read-only lock / audit / access control) above a row of compliance labels (GDPR, ISO 27001, SOC). Restrained.
- **AI image prompt:** _"A calm horizontal trust strip: four simple line icons (isolated stack, one-way read-only lock, audit checkmark, role-based access key) above a muted row of compliance labels (GDPR, ISO 27001, SOC), premium enterprise design, lots of whitespace, no photos."_
